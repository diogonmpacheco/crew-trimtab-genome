Welcome to **crew-trimtab-genome**, an open-source sandbox for systems-level genomic hypothesis generation, phenotype mapping, and cross-disciplinary biological pattern exploration.

---

## 🧭 Core Concept: The Trim Tab Principle

A **trim tab** is a small control surface on a ship’s rudder that can move a much larger system with minimal force. Buckminster Fuller used it as a metaphor for how small, well-placed interventions can redirect large systems.

In biology, small genetic variants, regulatory shifts, or metabolic constraints may cascade into large phenotypic outcomes when embedded in complex systems.

This project explores that idea computationally and conceptually.

---

## 🧬 Project Identity

**crew-trimtab-genome** is an open systems-biology research sandbox focused on:

- genomic interpretation (WGS and SNP-level data)
- phenotype correlation mapping
- mechanistic hypothesis generation
- literature integration
- multi-system biological interaction modeling

It is a structured notebook for exploring how genetics, environment, metabolism, and neurobiology interact as a single coupled system.

---

## 🧠 Motivation: Patient-Driven Systems Exploration

This project originated from independent analysis of **Whole Genome Sequencing (WGS)** data and the attempt to connect:

- observed physiological traits  
- neurocognitive and behavioral phenotypes  
- dietary and environmental responses  
- known genetic variants and metabolic pathways  

Rather than treating genetics as isolated markers, this framework treats it as a **dynamic interacting system**.

---

## 🔬 Scientific Scope & Boundaries

This repository is exploratory in nature.

1. **Hypothesis-Driven Content**  
   All models, connections, and interpretations are hypotheses unless explicitly supported by strong external evidence.

2. **Not Medical Advice**  
   This repository is not a diagnostic system, clinical tool, or source of medical guidance.

3. **Exploratory Knowledge System**  
   The goal is to organize fragmented scientific literature and observational signals into testable conceptual models.

---

## 🧪 Epistemic Principles

- falsifiability over certainty  
- transparency over authority  
- mechanistic reasoning over correlation alone  
- documentation of supporting and contradictory evidence  
- reproducibility of interpretation pipelines  

Negative results are valuable data.
